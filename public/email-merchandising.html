How recommendations are scored

A practical guide to why products appear in an email capsule.

1. Products must be promotable

  • Active and published in Shopify
  • At least 3 units available
  • A price, product image and working storefront link

Products failing these checks are excluded before scoring.

2. The signals

  • Commercial: revenue in the selected trading window
  • Stock: available units
  • Conversion: purchases or units relative to GA4 views
  • Underexposure: fewer views than the rest of the eligible range
  • Newness: strongest at launch, fading over 90 days
  • Feature history: whether it has appeared in a sent email
  • Margin: GP%; missing cost uses a neutral value

3. Your objective changes the mix

ObjectiveWhat it rewards
Balanced Mix25% commercial · 20% newness · 20% never featured · 15% low views · 10% stock · 10% margin
New In55% newness · 20% stock · 15% margin · 10% conversion
Underexposed40% low views · 25% conversion · 20% stock · 15% margin
Never Featured50% never featured · 20% stock · 20% commercial · 10% newness
Proven Performers50% commercial · 20% conversion · 15% stock · 15% margin

4. Repeat protection

  • 0–28 days after sending: excluded
  • 29–55 days: allowed with a fading penalty
  • 56+ days: no repeat penalty

Only emails Klaviyo confirms as sent start the cooldown. Drafts do not.

5. Making the capsule feel coherent

Each choice gets a strong lift for shared materials and style words, matching the campaign theme and season, compatible colours, related product types and a similar price position.

A diversity penalty discourages more than two of one product type or two of one colour, but a closely related product can still win rather than forcing an odd product into slot six.

Reading the score

The score is a relative opportunity score for this catalogue, objective and trading window—not a permanent product rating. Products are first ranked for the objective, then a cohesion lift is added as the six-product story is assembled.

displayed score = objective score × repeat multiplier + capsule cohesion

Pinning preserves a product when you regenerate, but it cannot override availability or the four-week lockout. Performance is reported after sending; currently it affects future recommendations through sent-email history rather than automatically retraining the weights.
